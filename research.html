Research

Project 1: Generative Modeling for Physical Systems

I investigate how generative models such as normalizing flows and score-based diffusion processes can be adapted to physical systems with strong constraints — such as conservation laws or geometry. The goal is to build physically plausible models for uncertainty quantification and sampling-based inference.

Project 2: Multiscale Transport Simulation

This project focuses on multiscale numerical methods for radiation transport, neutron diffusion, and Fokker-Planck equations. I aim to build efficient schemes that retain accuracy across scales and can be accelerated using modern GPU and sparse linear solvers.

Project 3: Graph-Based Representations of Material Behavior

I explore how graph neural networks and message passing architectures can represent spatially varying material behavior in simulations, with applications in homogenization, surrogate modeling, and real-time simulation control.

Other Research Interests

Beyond my main projects, I’m interested in broader intersections of computation, applied mathematics, and physical modeling:

  • Probabilistic numerics and uncertainty-aware simulation
  • Bayesian inverse problems in PDE-constrained systems
  • Machine learning for physics-informed inference
  • High-dimensional approximation and dimensionality reduction
  • Scientific software development and reproducibility
